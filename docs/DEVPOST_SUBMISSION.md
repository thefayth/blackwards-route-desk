# Devpost Submission Draft

## Project

**Auntie AI: Blackwards Route Desk**

## Tagline

Turn an opportunity into an owner-first route packet before your work moves.

## Track

Work and Productivity

## Description

Black creators are routinely asked to move quickly through opportunities while the terms doing the most work remain vague: who owns the result, where credit appears, what rights travel, how compensation works, and whether a partner receives access to the creator's audience.

Auntie AI: Blackwards Route Desk turns those visible terms into a practical route packet. A creator chooses the route type, summarizes the offer without private data, and marks compensation, rights, credit, audience access, and urgency. GPT-5.6 returns a structured signal — Proceed, Clarify, Protect, or Pass — followed by ownership checks, extraction signals, questions to ask before yes, one action that can be completed in 24 hours, and a receipt checklist.

The product does not approve contracts or provide legal advice. It makes the hidden decision surface legible before work, rights, data, or leverage move.

The live app uses the OpenAI Responses API with GPT-5.6, medium reasoning, strict JSON-schema output, no tools, `store: false`, and a second server-side schema check. A D1 ledger enforces small public quotas while storing only salted actor hashes, input hashes, model status, latency, timestamps, and counters. Creator summaries and generated packets are not persisted. Three synthetic packets remain available when live generation is unavailable.

Black2Africa existed before Build Week. The isolated Route Desk application, GPT-5.6 workflow, privacy guard, D1 ledger, synthetic cases, responsive interface, tests, social card, demo, and evidence package were newly built during the submission period with Codex.
